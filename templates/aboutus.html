The COVID-19 Clinical Trials Project

Purpose:

The COVID-19 Clinical Trials Dashboard was designed to give physicians and individuals a way to find COVID-19 trial information in a timely manner. We include an interactive map of the world that marks the locations of the different clincal trials. Zooming in on an area of the map brings up the specific locations. Clicking on one of these points, marked by a virus icon, brings up key information about the trial, including contact information. Other visualizations are provided that analyze aspects of the clinical trials. We explored developing a questionnaire that would be used to ask pertinent medical data from individuals that would then be used to direct them to clinical trials that they would be likely to be eligible to participate in. In the background, machine learning would be used to match the provided medical information with the trials. No PCI data is collected or stored since we do not ask for personal identification information.

Machine Learning:

Two medical studies were used as references: https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0110331 , "A Machine Learning Approach to Identify Clinical Trials Involving Nanodrugs and Nanodevices from ClinicalTrials.gov", and "Learning Eligibility in Cancer Clinical Trials Using Deep Neural Network" from the MDPI Journal for Applied Sciences. These two studies gave us the idea of using eligibility and exclusiion criteria in our machine learning effort. We decided to limit the scope of the project to those trials that involved drugs as interventions, and to further limit our data set to 5 the five drugs that are currently seen to show the most promise: Hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil) and chloroquine (Aralen), Remdesivir, Favilavir, Lopinavir-ritonavir, Convalescent plasma. Machine Learning in practice involves five key steps: 1)Get the data, 2) Clean, prepare, and manipulate the data, 3) Train the model, 4) Test the data, and 5) Improve.



We pulled the eligibility and exclusion criteria from the current set of clinical trials that contained these drugs in the clinical trial description and loaded them into the database. We prepared the data using pandas to remove extra words and created a csv file of lines of eligibility and exclusion criteris. We experimented with several machine learning models - Latent Dirichlet, Random Forest, Bag of Words and others until finally choosing Categorical Embedder, described in this article: "Categorical Embedder: Encoding Categorical Variables via Neural Networks" at https://medium.com/analytics-vidhya/categorical-embedder-encoding-categorical-variables-via-neural-networks-b482afb1409d .
